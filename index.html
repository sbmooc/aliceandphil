We’ve given a few tips on places to see and things to do around the area while you’re there.

Best beaches

There are a huge number and variety of beaches in the area, here are our top picks:

  • Calella de Palafrugell (Alice’s favourite in the world)
  • Sa riera (a classic costa bravan cove close to Begur)
  • Llafranc (good for a seafront lunch)
  • Playa del Castel (a really beachy beach for beach lovers)

Towns:

  • Begur (a wonderful town sat on the top of the hills straight up from the coast. A crumbling medieval castle overlooks a cosy town of squares for drinking and eating, Cuban inspired architecture and winding alleys of restaurants and shops)
  • Pals old town (an excellently maintained medieval town – for a low-key afternoon stroll)
  • Girona (an attractive city of pebble streets, with some excellent restaurants and ice cream shops and a filming location for a load of Game of Thrones (apparently)
  • Peratallada (alice hasn’t ever even suggested that I go there, so I can’t give you any insight, but the pictures look nice and apparently the bridesmaids really like this little catalonian village with great value lunch menus…and pottery)

Other activities

Walk up the hill at Torroella de Montpgri (affectionately known as the bishop, because of its resemblance to a bishop on his back, sticking up from the flat fields – there is a fort sat on top of this 300metre high hill. it’s a nice steep walk, with some nice views over the plains (where it mostly rains in Spain) and is a decent way to burn off some Paella. The website link says that the round walk from the town, up and down the bishop should take about 4 hours, i think that’s probably ambling pace, but gives you an idea)

Go kart at Karting Regencos (an Opie holiday classic. its an outdoor track, that doesn’t require booking and has a shack bar for those not driving. its located a couple of minutes outside of Begur by car. It’s a sharp turning off the main road, up a dirt track, but its worth it when you get there.

Wine tasting at mas oller (There are excellent wines all around this area and this producer will give you the tour of the vineyards and obviously a number of varieties to try – for around €17 - )

If you are staying for a bit longer and looking slightly further a field

  • Figueres (the home of the Salvador Dali museum…who once tried to buy our wedding venue with a few of his paintings)
